Sharpening Angle Chart
Typical bevel angles for the edge tools you sharpen most, plus a quick helper for figuring out where a micro-bevel lands.
ToolBevel angle
Steeper (higher-angle) edges are more durable but take more effort to push through the cut; shallower edges slice easier but chip or roll over faster in hard or gnarly wood. Many sharpeners grind the primary bevel at the low end of the range, then hone a 3–5° steeper micro-bevel for a durable, easy-to-maintain edge.

Micro-Bevel Helper

Enter your primary bevel angle and how many degrees steeper you want the micro-bevel, and this adds them up.
